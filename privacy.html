ClipReplay Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 8, 2026

ClipReplay is designed as a local publishing assistant for creators and teams. This policy explains what data is used by the tool.

Data we process

ClipReplay may process uploaded videos, captions, draft metadata, schedule data, connected account names, OAuth tokens, and publishing results needed to run the workflow.

How data is used

Data is used to save local drafts, adapt copy, display connected channels, upload media, and publish posts to accounts that you authorize.

Third-party services

When you connect YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, or other platforms, their APIs receive the content and metadata required to perform the publishing action you request.

Local storage

In local mode, ClipReplay stores configuration and workflow data on the operator's machine. The operator is responsible for protecting API keys, OAuth tokens, and uploaded media files.

Deletion

The operator can delete local uploaded files, drafts, tasks, and revoke third-party access through the connected platform account settings.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the ClipReplay operator who provided access to your workspace.
